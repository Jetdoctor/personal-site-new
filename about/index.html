Short bio

I've been playing video games for as long as I can remember, starting with consoles like the Sega Master System and the Atari 2600. Deep down I always knew I wanted a career doing what I loved, but the gaming industry in Turkey was virtually non-existent, so after high school I chose Software Engineering as the most viable path in, earning a full scholarship at Izmir University of Economics. During my degree, two of my student games were Greenlit on Steam.

Realizing my true passion lay in design rather than programming, I moved to the US for a Master's in Game Design at Full Sail University. Shortly before graduation I joined Sledgehammer Games as an Associate Technical Designer on Call of Duty: WWII, and later Sumo Digital in England, where I contributed to an unannounced AAA multiplayer shooter and Spyder for Apple Arcade, until COVID hit in early 2020.

Back in Turkey, I ran my own small technology venture for a few years, then moved toward what had quietly become my favorite part of every job: sitting between people, technology, and decisions. As an IT Business Analyst at CFN Kimya I built the company's Power BI reporting landscape, and I then moved to Hamburg for an MSc in Global Technology and Innovation Management at TU Hamburg, working alongside it in R&D change management at Olympus. My master's thesis explores how organizations respond strategically to AI at the business model level.

Games gave me my start; product and innovation management is where I've landed. And honestly, shipping something people love feels the same in both worlds.
